With over five years of production experience, I have utilized my creative storytelling and technical expertise to create VR experiences at Studio Syro. I am Co-Founder of the studio and serve as the Creative Producer of our team of artists and developers and together we created Tales From Soda Island a VR animated series. My leadership focused on fostering a collaborative and innovative culture motivating them to consistently pioneer new forms of entertainment in the VR space.

Long before Syro, I started pursuing a role as technical artist intern, working for Perilous Orbit on their Social Club VR title, they were known for one of the first billard VR games. From there, I continued to study and learn all I could about graphics and found various artists to idolize such as Keijiro Takahashi. While creating real-time art on my own time, I worked for e-TechServices as a Technical Sales Engineer learning business development and IT infrastructure. Not long after I worked for KWEST, an innovative AR start-up looking to expand on location based games. I served as their Technical Artist, undercovering mobile development limitations and then later became the Producer for their team.

Now we have arrived at one of the most interesting turnpoints in my journey… The start of Studio Syro.

One summer, I was approached by my close friend, Neek, who was contemplating undertaking a music video project. During our discussion, we analyzed everything conceptually – the story, the 3D technical aspects, the potential use of real-time engines, and more. Eventually, he agreed that we could do it, so we assembled a team of artists. However, these were not ordinary artists, but VR Quill Artists. This project represented one of the earliest use cases for VR as a primary production tool, and we were ambitious enough to make it all work. As the Producer of the project, I focused on coordinating with all these new members and the development teams we engaged. Together, we created an incredible video for the artist we collaborated with. More importantly, we formed a team of some of the most talented VR painters and animators to date. This marked the beginning of Studio Syro.

After creating that video we were approached by Oculus with an opportunity to create a series for their Quest headsets utilizing Quill. As a team, we developed “Tales From Soda Island” based on some existing work created by our Creative Director, Simone Fougnier (also known as Funilabs).

Over the next three years, I managed this studio alongside our dedicated team. We continued to expand on the series and to develop a variety of projects. I found myself immersing in the full scope of what it takes to manage this unique company.

Productions, whether crafted for entertainment or engineered for software, require collaboration of creative and technical minds and that synergy is where the innovation stems from. My role is to foster an environment where a team can perform at their best. Our collective efforts and the time spent is just as important as our end goal.
